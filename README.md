**Project Overview**
This project focuses on analyzing customer churn behavior in a banking dataset of ~10,000 customers. The objective is to identify key drivers of churn, understand customer behavior, and provide actionable insights to improve customer retention and engagement.

**Objectives**
Identify factors contributing to customer churn
Analyze customer behavior based on demographics and account details
Evaluate product usage and engagement patterns
Provide data-driven recommendations to reduce churn

**Dataset Description**

The dataset includes:

Customer demographics (Age, Gender, Geography)
Account details (Balance, Tenure, Number of Products)
Behavioral indicators (IsActiveMember, HasCrCard)
Target variable: Exited (Churn Status)

**📊 Dashboard Features**
**1. Executive Overview**
Overall churn rate
Customer distribution
Key KPIs
**2. Customer Segmentation**
Churn by Age, Gender, Geography
Salary and Balance segmentation
**3. Behavior & Drivers**
Churn by Activity Status
Churn by Number of Products
Product affinity analysis
**4. Customer Deep Dive (Drill-through)**
Segment-level analysis
Interactive filtering for detailed insights

**Key Insights**
Inactive customers have significantly higher churn rates
Customers with 2 products show the lowest churn (optimal engagement)
High churn observed in age group 46+
Germany region shows higher churn despite higher balances
Salary and balance have minimal impact on churn compared to behavioral factors

**Recommendations**
Improve customer engagement through personalized communication
Focus on converting single-product users to multi-product users
Target high-risk segments (inactive, older customers, specific regions)
Enhance customer experience and simplify product usage
Implement predictive churn models for early intervention

**Tools & Technologies**
Power BI (Dashboard & Visualization)
SQL (Data Analysis & Queries)
Excel (Data Cleaning & Preparation)

**How to Use**
Download the .pbix file from this repository
Open it in Power BI Desktop
Use slicers and filters to explore insights
Navigate through pages for detailed analysis

**Conclusion**
This project highlights that customer churn is primarily driven by behavioral factors such as engagement and product usage rather than financial capacity. Targeted strategies based on these insights can significantly improve retention.
